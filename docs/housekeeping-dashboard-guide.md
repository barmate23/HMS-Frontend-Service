# Housekeeping Dashboard Guide

## Purpose

The Housekeeping Dashboard gives a live view of room readiness, pending housekeeping work, maintenance blockers, audit checks, and room-level status. It helps the housekeeping team decide which rooms are ready, which rooms need action, and where attention is required before rooms can be released for guests.

## 1. Room Readiness Board

This is the main summary panel for housekeeping operations.

- **Housekeeping Command**: Identifies this screen as the central housekeeping control view.
- **Room Readiness Board**: Shows that the dashboard is focused on room health and readiness.
- **Readiness Percentage**: The circular indicator shows the percentage of rooms that are ready based on housekeeping status, audits, and blockers.
- **Ready Label**: Confirms that rooms are currently in a ready state when the readiness percentage is complete.

In the screenshot, readiness is **100%**, meaning all tracked rooms are considered ready based on the current dashboard rules.

## 2. Summary KPI Cards

These cards give quick operational counts.

### Ready Rooms

Shows how many rooms are clean, inspected, and ready for guest use.

Example: **2 Ready Rooms** means two rooms can be treated as available from a housekeeping readiness point of view.

### Need Service

Shows how many rooms still need housekeeping service, cleaning, or attention.

Example: **0 Need Service** means no room is currently waiting for cleaning/service.

### Blocked/DND

Shows rooms that cannot be serviced at the moment.

This can include:

- Blocked rooms
- Do Not Disturb rooms
- Rooms unavailable for housekeeping action

Example: **0 Blocked/DND** means there are no rooms currently blocked or marked DND.

### Open Tasks

Shows housekeeping tasks that are still pending.

These may include:

- Room cleaning
- Inspection
- Linen replacement
- Minibar check
- Lost and found follow-up

Example: **2 Open Tasks** means two housekeeping tasks still need action.

### Repair Issues

Shows maintenance issues reported from housekeeping.

Examples:

- AC issue
- Plumbing issue
- Broken light
- Door lock issue
- Furniture or fixture damage

Example: **1 Repair Issue** means one maintenance-related blocker is currently open.

### SOP Checks

Shows the number of standard operating procedure checkpoints being tracked or completed.

Examples:

- Bed linen check
- Bathroom check
- Amenities check
- Dusting check
- Floor cleanliness
- Final inspection

Example: **6 SOP Checks** means six checklist points are part of the current readiness tracking.

## 3. Attention Queue

This section highlights issues that need supervisor attention.

### Vacant Dirty

Rooms that are vacant but still dirty. These are high priority because they may be needed for new arrivals.

Example: **0 Vacant Dirty** means no vacant dirty rooms are waiting.

### Maintenance Blockers

Rooms affected by maintenance issues that can prevent release.

Example: **1 Maintenance Blocker** means one issue needs repair or clearance before the room can be fully released.

### Stored Lost & Found

Lost and found items that have been recorded and stored.

Example: **1 Stored Lost & Found** means one item is currently stored and tracked.

## 4. Team Load

This section shows the current workload for housekeeping staff.

### Pending Tasks

Tasks waiting to be started or completed.

Example: **2 Pending Tasks** means two tasks are still pending.

### In Progress

Tasks currently being worked on by staff.

Example: **0 In Progress** means no task is currently marked active.

### Staff Profiles

Number of housekeeping staff profiles available in the system.

Example: **2 Staff Profiles** means two staff members are configured or tracked.

## 5. Audit Readiness

This section shows whether housekeeping audit and SOP checks are active.

### Active SOP

Shows the current SOP schedule or audit frequency.

Example: **Daily Active SOP** means daily housekeeping SOP checks are active.

### Checkpoints

Number of checklist items included in the audit.

Example: **6 Checkpoints** means six inspection points are checked.

### Rooms Tracked

Number of rooms currently included in the audit/readiness view.

Example: **2 Rooms Tracked** means two rooms are being monitored in this dashboard.

## 6. Floor Section

Rooms are grouped floor-wise.

### Floor Header

Shows the floor number and number of rooms on that floor.

Example: **Floor 1 - 2 Rooms** means two rooms are listed under Floor 1.

## 7. Room Cards

Each room card gives room-level housekeeping information.

### Room Number

Shows the room number.

Example: **101** or **102**.

### Room Type

Shows the room category.

Example:

- Deluxe
- Luxury

### Housekeeping Status

Shows the current cleaning/readiness status of the room.

Examples:

- **Occupied Clean**: Guest is in the room and the room is clean.
- **Vacant Clean**: Room is empty and clean.

### Room Indicators

Small badges show room-related counts or alerts.

Examples:

- Task count
- Repair issue count
- Queue/attention item count
- SOP check count
- Clear status

### Assigned Staff / Inspector

Shows the staff member linked to the room.

Example: **Akshay** is assigned or responsible for inspection/housekeeping status.

### Verified / Ready Icon

A final check icon means the room has passed required readiness or audit checks.

## How to Read This Dashboard

1. Start with the readiness percentage to understand overall room health.
2. Check KPI cards for ready rooms, service needs, blockers, tasks, and repairs.
3. Review the Attention Queue for urgent issues.
4. Use Team Load to understand pending work and staff capacity.
5. Check Audit Readiness to confirm SOP tracking is active.
6. Review floor-wise room cards for individual room status.
